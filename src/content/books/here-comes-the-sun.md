Following the infrastructure optimism of [Abundance](abundance), <i>Here Comes the Sun</i> doubles down on the “we can do it” mentality. I discovered this book on a whim perusing my local book store ([porter square books is the best](https://portersquarebooks.com/)). I am glad I found it.

Solar power is now cheap. Large-scale wind projects, when they overcome local opposition, can produce significant electrons: There will soon be [100GW of energy in North Sea](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jan/26/uk-among-10-countries-to-build-100gw-wind-power-grid-in-north-sea). Small modular reactors are becoming practical in the [AI age](https://www.ft.com/content/025cfe98-9d3d-4adb-8081-4fdf5950b0f8).

Oil and gas are antiques, akin to the horse and buggy.

McKibben has an [excellent substack](https://billmckibben.substack.com/) that I now subscribe to. His posts are long but valuable.
